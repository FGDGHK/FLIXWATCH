AMERICAN REVOLUTION

Episode 1: Seven Years War

Great Britain and France both seek to establish their presence in North America 1756, however, at the expense of the other.

Episode 2: Stamp Act

In the aftermath of the Seven Years War that ended 1763, Britain is left in debt. In 1765, they sought to impose a tax as a source of revenue. However, it is protested against by the colonists.

Episode 3: The Declatory Act

Representatives from the thirteen colonies gather to assemble the Stamp Act Congress in response to the Stamp Act 1765. When British parliament was faced refusal to comply with the act and protests, they agreed to repeal the Stamp Act with the condition that the Declatory Act passes.

Episode 4: Boston Massacre

The Boston Massacre was a confrontation on March 5 1770, where 9 British soldiers shot 5 people in a 300 to 400 person crowd who were harassing them . Within weeks of the incident there was an investigation and a propaganda battle between Bostons patriots and loyalists.

Episode 5: Boston Tea Party

The Boston Tea Party was a political and mercantile protest by the Sons of Liberty in Boston, Massachusettes on December 16 1773. The target of the protest was the Tea Act of May 10 1773. They disguised as Indigineous Americans and destroyed a shipment of tea.

Episode 6: The Intolerable Acts

A series of punitive acts were passed by British Parliament in 1774 to punish Massachusette colonists for their defiance to the Tea Act of 1773. The colonists viewed the acts as a violation of their rights and made more colonists turn agaisnt british rule.

Episode 7: A new challenger appears! Thomas Paine!

Thomas Paine is a political activist, philosopher, political theorist, and revolutionary. Starting from the beginning of his journey on February 9 1737 in England, to when he wrote Common Sense, which inspired the colonists to declare independence from Great Britain.

Episode 8: Battle of Lexington and Concord

April 19 1775, British soldiers move to seize and destroy colonial militia supplies. Lexington's battle ended as a skirmish with 8 militiamen dead and 1 British casualty. The battle of Concord had the British retreating to Charlestown with the militia having only 90 killed and wouned compared to the 250 British soldiers

Episode 9: Battle of Bunker Hill

June 13 1775, the colonial forces learn that British forces were planning to fortify the hills around Boston. In response the colonial troops occupy Bunker and Breed's Hill. June 17 the British forces attacked and left them in control of the Peninsula, however, not without much loss.

Episode 10, Season Finale: Declaration of Independence

Congress appoints a "Committee of Five" to draft a declaration on June 11 1776. On July 2 held a unanimous 12 votes for independence. The Declaration of Independence was approved on July 4, and that announced the separation of the 13 colonies from Great Britain.
